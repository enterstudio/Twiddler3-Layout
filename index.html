Looking for a chorded-keyboard layout standard

Why should we learn some chording layout that makes sense only to the author? A better idea would be to take a part in the build process. And if it's too late, having a chance to read the story is the next best way to get the idea.

Every single decision should be clearly justified to avoid having to keep tabs on unclear points requiring further explanation. As this is not the case even in The TabSpace Twiddler Key Assignments by Brandon Craig Rhodes, the original author of the currently accepted standard layout for this device, chances are a systematic study of all the possible combinations could lead to some alternate and possibly better solution.
